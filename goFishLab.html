Go Fish (arrays) Lab

Objective

The objective of this lab is to create the beginnings for a java-based go-fish game. We DO NOT expect a fully functioning go-fish game. What we do expect is that you create a main function and various methods that will accomplish the following goals:

  • Create variables representing the POND, the PLAYER and the COMPUTER
    (Hint: you may want to declare these as final ints and initialize them to 0, 1 and 2 respectivley. You could use these values later in your program to indicate if a card is located in the POND or in the hand of the PLAYER or COMPUTER)
  • Place all cards in the deck into the pond (indicate that all cards are located in the pond)
  • Get a card from the pond and place it in the user's or computer's hand (find a card in the pond and indicate that it is now in the hand of the user or the computer)
  • Show the cards that are in the deck (we want to see all 52 cards with their suit and rank)
    See the hint regarding getCardName()
  • Show the cards that are in the hands of the PLAYER and COMPUTER

Hints

  • You can implement this lab without the use of two-dimensional arrays
  • It may be easier to create a getCardName() method to determine the card name from the position in the deck. This function will be very familiar if you remember the change-maker lab
